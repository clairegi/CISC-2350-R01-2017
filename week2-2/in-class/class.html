Already one of the most powerful storms ever recorded, Irma could become one
of the most destructive as well, depending on its path, and officials from Turks and Caicos to Florida pleaded with
people to heed advisories to evacuate to shelters and higher ground. The National Hurricane Center described the
hurricane as “potentially catastrophic.”

The storm made direct hits on Barbuda, St. Barthélemy, St. Martin, Anguilla and the
British Virgin Islands, and raked the United States Virgin Islands and Puerto Rico with hurricane-force wind and torrential
rain. Gaston Browne, prime minister of Antigua and Barbuda, said Irma had destroyed 95 percent of the structures on Barbuda, an
island with about 1,600 people.

The authorities confirmed that the hurricane had killed at least one person in
Antigua and Barbuda; one on Anguilla, a British possession; and two in French territory, which includes St. Barthélemy
and the northern part of St. Martin. Another died in Puerto Rico while preparing for the storm.
